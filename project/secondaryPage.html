What is a 72-Hour Emergency Kit?

A 72-hour emergency kit, also known as a "go bag" or "bug-out bag," is a portable collection of essential items designed to sustain an individual or family for three days during a disaster or emergency. This kit typically includes food, water, first aid supplies, clothing, tools, and other necessities. The 72-hour timeframe is based on the assumption that it may take up to three days for emergency responders to reach those affected by a disaster, making it crucial for individuals to have the necessary supplies to survive on their own until help arrives.

What should a 72-hour emergency kit contain?

  • Water

    At least one gallon per person per day.

  • Food

    Non-perishable items like canned goods, protein bars, dried fruits, and nuts.

  • Clothing and Bedding

    A change of clothes, sturdy shoes, and a sleeping bag or blanket for each person.

  • Tools and Supplies

    A flashlight with extra batteries, multi-purpose tool, matches in a waterproof container, and a whistle.

  • First Aid Kit

    Basic supplies like bandages, antiseptic wipes, tweezers, and any necessary prescription medications.

  • Sanitation

    Moist towelettes, garbage bags, and plastic ties for personal sanitation.

  • Personal Documents

    Copies of important documents such as insurance policies, identification, and bank account records in a waterproof, portable container.

  • Miscellaneous Items

    Local maps, a cell phone with chargers, and cash in small denominations.

Create An Emergency Plan

Creating a family emergency plan is crucial because it ensures that all family members know what to do and where to go in case of a disaster. This preparation reduces panic, minimizes confusion, and increases the chances of everyone staying safe and reuniting quickly. A well-thought-out plan includes communication strategies, designated meeting spots, and essential emergency contacts, helping families navigate the chaos of unexpected events more effectively.
