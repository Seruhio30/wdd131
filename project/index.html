Are You Ready?

A 72-hour emergency kit, often referred to as a "bug-out bag" or "go bag," is a portable collection of essential items designed to help individuals survive for 72 hours during a disaster or emergency.

The 72-hour duration is based on the assumption that it may take up to three days for emergency services to reach people affected by disasters.

Where can I find shelters and assistance in my area after a disaster?

After a disaster, you can find shelters and assistance in your area by contacting local emergency services They often set up temporary shelters and provide essential services.

Earthquakes can strike suddenly, without warning. Ensure your home is secured and have an emergency plan in place.

Floods can happen quickly. Know your evacuation routes and have a plan to protect your important documents and belongings.

Hurricanes bring strong winds and heavy rain. Secure outdoor items, have a supply kit, and stay informed about local warnings.

Landslides can occur with little notice. Be aware of the signs and ensure your home is not in a high-risk area.
